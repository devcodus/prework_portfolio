Music, Theory, and Production

My interest in music has been pretty life-long. My first major impact was listening to I’m Blue by Eiffel 65 on the radio. That moment foretold my future foray into music exploration.

Slowly but surely, I started making a beeline to the source: Music Production itself. After some false starts and fits and go’s, I finally make a whole complete track in one sitting in major part thanks to my Music Mentor/Teacher: Enoch.

Looking forward, my latest chapter in my Music Saga intends to build myself up right: focusing on learning a handful of instruments while teaching myself music theory, as continue trying my hand at music production with DAW’s. The challenges I foresee include: being consistent, being consistent, being consistent, and learning to learn DAW’s without the benefit of a personal one-on-one tutor. I told myself that during my coding bootcamp busy-ness I would hold off on learning the DAW until I could hire another personal 1-on-1 tutor, however now I feel as though it would be better if I could teach myself intermediary skills that will make those future apprenticeship sessions much more productive and focused.

The aforementioned skills might include music theory and rudimentary instrument playing, as well as making the basic templates of my go-to music genres inside of the DAW…

my coding story

My interest in coding started in 2014 when I started to hear about a technology on twitter called Ethereum. It seemed to promise so much that would catapult society’s current ills, such as privacy incursions. It was a buzzing field with a lot of interest starting to be generated.

Thus my affinity for the tech field as a participator rather than a consumer, was born.

Eventually I heard about coding bootcamps and decided it would be a good idea to join one. In 2017 I joined a bootcamp called Coding Dojo. I did good enough, although not as stellar as I hoped. I attribute this to my still coming out of my social shell, thus my attention was disproportionately taken over by social cares, rather than investing 100% into my tech deep dive.

At the end of the bootcamp, I got a job with a friend that I ended up regretting. I feel as though if I got a job that I got on my own, rather than tagging along with my friend, I would have acquired a job that was a better fit.

This bosses and work had me hating my job. (I was basically enlisted to code my own replacement.) I had ascribed my hatred to my lack of collegiate computer engineer knowledge. Unfortunately, I ascribed too much importance to this hatred that wouldn’t go away, and took it as a sign that I should simply leave the field altogether.

Life, however, had different plans for me. Eventually, life brought me on a bee-line right back square to the coding field, this time to Coding Temple.

I have a deep feeling that this time it will be different.

This time, I’m not being distracted by social cares.

This time around, I am coming with a better sense of emotional intelligence and assimilation of handling any feelings of hatred.

This time, I’m committing myself to a deep dive in the web dev field.
